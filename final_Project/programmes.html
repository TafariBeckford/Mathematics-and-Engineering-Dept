BSc. in Mathematics(With Minor)

The Department of Mathematics and Statistics provides undergraduate and postgraduate instruction and training in pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and statistics. A Bachelor of Science degree is available in Mathematics, as well as joint BSc degrees in Mathematics and Physics, Economics and Mathematics, Chemistry and Mathematics, and Computer Science and Mathematics.In addition, there is a minor in Mathematics and a minor in Statistics.

Mathematics Admission requirements

• CAPE Math (U1&U2) – Grades 1,2,3
• CAPE/CSEC English A – Grades 1,2,3
• Three (3) Extra CAPE/CSEC Subjects (Grades 1,2,3)

**If you have good passes in CXC Math (Grades 1 and 2) you may still apply under the NCU General Studies Programme. However, you will require (at minimum) an additional year to satisfactorily complete the required pre-calculus courses, along with our evaluation of your demonstrated work ethic before acceptance into the “Foundation Year”.
NB: Acceptance into the “Foundation Year” program does not mean matriculation in the BSc. Programme. Matriculation into the BSc. Mathematics program (Years 2-4) requires that ALL Foundation Year math courses be passed with a minimum “B average”. AND A Minimum Cumulative Foundation Year GPA of 3.0 must be achieved.
If you complete your foundation year without meeting these requirements, you MUST do so within one year OR regrettably, you will be withdrawn from our programme. Internship/Practicum & Service Learning You are required to do an internship or practicum for minimum period of (3) months at a business firm or school, working on a mathematics application or program. You will practice service learning as you contribute tangibly to your organizations growth & development through innovation.

BSc. in Mathematics (Pure & Applied)

Mathematics is a universal language that explains the currents of the ocean, string theory, the spiral of a snail’s shell or the growth of a fern. When you study pure and applied mathematics, you enter a field that has both a rich history and many future career possibilities. As a mathematician, you’ll design and analyze mathematical models and develop systems for testing and evaluation. In essence, you will use mathematics to find creative solutions for systems such as communications, software development, encryption technologies, banking and drug testing.Through labs and lectures, you will learn to use professional software tools for mathematics and data analysis applications. You’ll also learn to think in the abstract and fine-tune your analytical skills.

BSc. in Mathematics (Education Concentration)

The MICA program provide student with the area of concentration in applied and computational mathematics, mathematics education, pure mathematics or statistics, or they can choose to have no area of concentration. •Renewable Energy Systems Concentration. •Process Control Systems Concentration. •Communication Systems Concentration.

BSc. in Mathematics (Actuarial Science Concentration)

This combined program offers you a direct path to an actuarial career with a financial sector specialization. In the program, you do all the Actuarial Mathematics coursework: studying statistics, probability, and learning mathematical models to solve problems involving uncertainty.

BSc.in Electrical and Electronic Engineering)

Electrical engineering is the branch of the engineering profession responsible for the planning and design of systems using electricity, either as a form of energy (so-called heavy-current or electrical engineering) or as a means of representing, storing, and transmitting information (light-current or electronic engineering).

BSc.in Computer Systems Engineering

Computer Systems Engineering includes aspects of computer science, electronics engineering and software engineering. It looks at how the hardware and software components of computing systems and computer controlled systems are designed, constructed, implemented and maintained.The Computer Systems Engineering programme is designed to expose students to a combination of theory and practical work, in order to allow students to diagnose real life problems and develop a variety of solutions.

Engineering Admission requirements

• CAPE Math (U1&U2) – Grades 1,2,3
• CAPE/CSEC English A – Grades 1,2,3
• Three (3) Extra CAPE/CSEC Subjects (Grades 1,2,3)
* Either CAPE (U1&2) in Electrical & Electronic Technology or Geometric & Mechanical Engineering Drawing (GMED) may be used as a substitute for CAPE Physics (U1 &U2).
**If you have good passes in CXC Math & Physics passes (Grades 1 and 2) you may still apply under the NCU General Studies Programme. However, you will require (at minimum) an additional
year to satisfactorily complete the required pre-calculus courses, along with our evaluation of your demonstrated work ethic before acceptance into the “Foundation Year”.
NB: Acceptance into the Foundation Year does not mean acceptance into our BEng Degree Programs. For Matriculation into our BEng programs ALL foundation year courses must be passed
AND you must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0. If you complete your foundation year without meeting these requirements, you must do so within one (1) year.
